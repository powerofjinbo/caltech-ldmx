# 5 GeV Clean-Muon Baseline

This note records the current standalone Geant4 baseline after the project was
narrowed to a single clean-muon configuration.

## Scope

- Primary beam: one `mu-` per Geant4 event.
- Beam energy: `5 GeV`.
- Geometry: 40 LYSO layers, 10 crystals per layer, each crystal
  `1 cm x 1 cm x 10 cm`.
- Output prefix: `output/muon_5gev_40layer`.
- Manual extra-particle generator modes are disabled and their macros were
  removed.

The purpose of this baseline is to measure the ordinary MIP-like muon response
and the natural extra-activity tail generated by the Geant4 physics list.

## Regeneration Commands

```sh
source ../setup_geant4_11_4_1.sh
cmake --build build -j
./build/muon_active_target macros/run_muon.mac
python3 analysis/summarize_events.py
python3 analysis/plot_basic.py
python3 analysis/track_event_display.py --select typical
python3 analysis/track_event_display.py --select high-extra
```

## Baseline Numbers

Generated with the default lightweight `EM` physics list, 1000 events.

```text
Events: 1000
1 MIP/layer: 9.094 MeV
Mean primary muon edep: 363.769 MeV
Mean total edep: 476.043 MeV
Mean extra edep: 112.274 MeV
Mean outgoing muon KE: 4.514 GeV
```

The result is consistent with the expected MIP scale: about `9.1 MeV/layer`,
or `40 x 9.1 MeV ~= 364 MeV` of primary-muon deposition across 40 layers.

## Interpretation

This is not a background sample and not a dark-matter signal sample. It is the
ordinary response of the active target to a 5 GeV muon. The useful outputs are
the MIP/layer calibration, the outgoing-muon energy distribution, and the
non-primary activity tails that future veto definitions must understand.
